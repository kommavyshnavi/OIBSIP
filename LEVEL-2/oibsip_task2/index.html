Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam : The Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, also known as the Missile Man of India, was a renowned aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. Dr. Kalam's dedication, humility, and vision for India's future made him a beloved luminary and an inspirational leader.

Biography and Achievements

Dr. Kalam graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960 and joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was the project director for SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle. Dr. Kalam was instrumental in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earning him the title "Missile Man of India."

In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected the 11th President of India, where he served with distinction until 2007. Known for his integrity and dedication to the country, he was widely respected by all political parties. After his presidency, he continued to inspire millions through his lectures, books, and interactions with students across the country.
